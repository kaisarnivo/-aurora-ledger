1 · Executive Summary

Aurora Ledger is a Cosmos-SDK Layer 1 purpose-built for regulated RWAs. Developed by NivoNexus, it embeds compliance logic, custody attestations, and quantum-safe primitives directly into the protocol while remaining fully open-source. Aurora enables asset managers to launch tokenized funds, metals, credit products, or real-estate SPVs using a single governance/gas token (AUR) with transparent economics.

  • Compliance-by-design: investor eligibility, jurisdiction policies, freeze/pause controls.
  • Custody transparency: oracle-verified attestations before supply changes.
  • PQC roadmap: hybrid Dilithium/Falcon + secp256k1 signatures.
  • Automation-first: OpenClaw agents deploy validators, configure compliance, monitor 24/7.
  • Tenant subchains: dedicated execution environments inheriting settlement security.

2 · Market Landscape & Opportunity

The tokenized asset market is projected to exceed $10T by 2030. Institutional adoption is gated by regulatory uncertainty, fragmented tooling, and security risks. Aurora Ledger addresses these pain points by providing compliance primitives, auditable custody proofs, and automated operations in a single chain.

  • Regulators: auditable, immutable compliance logs.
  • Asset managers: template-driven issuance instead of bespoke code.
  • Investors: predictable, gas-efficient interactions secured by open-source tooling.
  • Custodians: built-in incentives for timely attestations and audits.

3 · Vision & Value Proposition

Aurora Ledger aims to be the compliance-native settlement layer for institutional RWAs.

  • Trust: deterministic enforcement of policies reduces manual reviews.
  • Transparency: open data on audit cadence, supply movements, oracle performance.
  • Automation: agent-driven ops eliminate human error while preserving oversight.
  • Future readiness: PQC and modular subchains adapt to new jurisdictions and assets.

4.1 Core Modules

  • rwa_registry: metadata, legal references, custodians, audit cadence.
  • compliance_core: KYC/KYB hashes, jurisdiction flags, investor tiers, freeze controls.
  • asset_mint_burn: issuance/redemption gated by custody quorum + compliance approval.
  • oracle_bridge: integrates OCR feeds & custody attestations; slashes stale operators.
  • migration_bridge: ERC-1400 + ICS-20 adapters for cross-chain movement.
  • subchain_manager: registers tenant subchains and enforces guardrails.

4.2 Automation Layer

OpenClaw agents handle validator bootstrapping, compliance template deployment, monitoring, and alerting. Hooks integrate with Grafana/Prometheus and ticketing systems.

4.3 Tenant Subchains

Asset managers can request dedicated subchains (Cosmos consumer chains or optimistic rollups). Subchains inherit compliance policies from the main L1 and settle via shared security.

5 · Compliance & Custody Framework

  1. Attestation Flow: investors complete KYC/KYB with approved providers; hashed credentials stored on-chain.
  2. Transfer Execution: every operation checks compliance rules (eligibility, caps, lockups).
  3. Custody Controls: oracle feeds ingest vault balances/NAV; mint/burn requires attestation quorum.

6 · Security & PQC Roadmap

  • Hybrid signatures (secp256k1 + Dilithium/Falcon) for validators and key contracts.
  • PQC TLS on P2P links, roadmap for PQ light clients once specs finalize.
  • HSM-compatible key management with rotation policies enforced by automation agents.
  • Independent audits for chain, bridges, and automation before mainnet.
